Privacy Policy

Your voice stays on your machine. Period.

Last Updated: January 11, 2025

The Short Version

MurmurTone processes your voice 100% locally on your computer. We don't collect, store, or transmit your voice data, transcriptions, or usage patterns. The only network communication is a one-time license validation when you activate a Pro license.

1. Our Privacy Commitment

MurmurTone was built with privacy as a core principle, not an afterthought. We believe your voice data is deeply personal and should never leave your control. That's why:

  • All speech recognition happens locally on your Windows computer
  • Your voice recordings are never transmitted to any server
  • Your transcriptions stay on your machine and are never uploaded
  • No analytics, tracking, or telemetry is built into the application
  • The app works completely offline after initial setup

3. Local Data Storage

MurmurTone stores the following data locally on your computer in %APPDATA%\MurmurTone:

  • Settings: Your preferences (model selection, hotkey, audio settings)
  • Speech models: Downloaded AI models for transcription (~75MB-1.5GB)
  • Custom vocabulary: Words you've added for improved recognition
  • License file: Your Pro license activation (if purchased)
  • Log files: Application logs for troubleshooting (can be deleted)

You can delete all local data by uninstalling MurmurTone or removing the %APPDATA%\MurmurTone folder.

4. Network Communications

MurmurTone makes network requests only in these specific situations:

Model Downloads (First Use)

When you first use MurmurTone, it downloads the speech recognition model from Hugging Face's servers. This is a one-time download. After this, the app works completely offline.

License Activation (Pro Users)

When you activate a Pro license, MurmurTone contacts our license server to validate your purchase. This transmits:

  • Your license key
  • A machine identifier (hardware hash)

No voice data, transcriptions, or usage information is ever transmitted.

AI Text Cleanup (Optional)

If you enable the AI text cleanup feature with Ollama, that processing also happens 100% locally. Ollama runs on your machine - no cloud services are involved.

5. Third-Party Services

Hugging Face (Model Downloads)

Speech recognition models are downloaded from Hugging Face. Their privacy policy applies to the download process. See: huggingface.co/privacy

Gumroad (Payment Processing)

If you purchase a Pro license, payment is processed by Gumroad. Your payment information is handled by Gumroad according to their privacy policy. See: gumroad.com/privacy

We receive: your email address and order details. We do NOT receive your payment card information.

6. Children's Privacy

MurmurTone is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children.

7. Data Security

Since we don't collect or transmit your voice data or transcriptions, there's no risk of this data being breached from our servers. Your data security depends on your local computer's security.

For Pro license activation, we use industry-standard HTTPS encryption for the license validation request.

8. Your Rights

Because MurmurTone doesn't collect personal data beyond license activation, traditional data rights (access, correction, deletion) apply primarily to:

  • Email address: Contact us to update or delete your email from our license records
  • Local data: You can delete all local data by removing %APPDATA%\MurmurTone
  • License deactivation: Contact us to deactivate your license from a machine

9. Changes to This Policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top. For significant changes, we will provide notice through the application or via email (for Pro users).

Our commitment to local processing and not collecting voice data will not change.

10. Contact Us

If you have questions about this privacy policy or MurmurTone's privacy practices:

  • Email: support@murmurtone.com
  • GitHub: Open an issue

Privacy by Design

MurmurTone's architecture makes privacy violations technically impossible. Your voice is processed by AI models running on YOUR computer. There's no server to hack, no cloud to breach, no data to leak. That's not just a policy - it's how the software works.
